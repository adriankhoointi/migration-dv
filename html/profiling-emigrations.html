Analysis on Gender Profiles of Emigrants

Who are they?

This is a diverging stacked bar chart that illustrastes the yearly distribution of emigrants of South-East Asian countries based on their gender, from 1990-2020. The indicator used for count is countries in the South-Eastern Asia region to countries with the "High-Income Economies" status, according to World Bank's country classifications.

To use this visualisation, hover to view details of a particular bar segment, and click on the years in Legend to visualise the data for that particular year. Data for year 2020 is visualised by default.

Context

Due to prevalent gender discrepancies in South-East Asia, men and women experience varying degrees of fulfillment concerning their rights in social, economic, cultural, and political aspects.
Consequently, this environment will contribute to a situation where one gender or both are more motivated to emigrate, and thus exacerbating the phenomenon of brain drain.

The emigration count of Male against Female during a particlar year for South-East Asian countries is plotted on the chart above.

A significant difference in the number of male and female emigrants from a country may suggest underlying issues or dynamics within that nation at the time.
